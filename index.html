Rechargeable batteries LiPo, LiFePO4, NiMh LSD

The comparison of capacity, voltage and current under the load typical for the powerful torches.

Conditions

I used in the test the batteries with the almost the same capacity: noname LiPo 18650, almost new LiFePO4 of A123 System in the same size and a block of the three connected Eneloops (NiMh LSD) of AAA size.

It was obvious LiPo would be best in any mean, but it may not be seen as the choice in the cold weather - the low temperature kills the battery pretty soon.

500ma discharge results

Eneloops stayed alive 1.5 hours with the average voltage 3.3v and the capacity 790mAh

Conclusion

LiPo is out of the contest during the winter because it could be damaged by the low temperature but it is absolute winner in warm conditions. Eneloops and LiFePo4 are almost the same - capacity and voltage are very close, but LiFePo4 requires its own charger and can not be replaced with the ordinal non-rechargabel batteries.
